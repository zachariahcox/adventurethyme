## Ingredients
3 tablespoons olive oil  
1 medium onion, minced (about 1 cup)  
1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes  
1 bunch greens, ribs removed, leaves chopped into 2-inch squares  
1 cup low-sodium chicken broth  
3T Apple cider vinegar

Note: for thicker greens like collards or kale, increase cooking time by up to 10 minutes, add 1 cup water  
  
## Instructions

1. Heat 1 tablespoons oil in Dutch oven or non-stick skillet over medium heat until shimmering.  
1. Add onion and cook, stirring frequently, until softened and beginning to brown, 4 to 5 minutes.  
1. Add pepper flakes; cook until garlic is fragrant, about 1 minute.
1. Add half of greens and stir until beginning to wilt, about 1 minute.  
1. Add remaining greens, broth, water; quickly cover pot and reduce heat to medium-low.  
1. Cook, stirring occasionally, until greens are tender:  
    25 to 35 minutes for kale  
    35 to 45 minutes for collards.  
    10 to 20 minutes for cabbage collards, or dino kale, or other thin greens  
1. Remove lid and increase heat to medium-high.  
1. Cook, stirring occasionally, until most of liquid has evaporated (bottom of pot will be almost dry and greens will begin to sizzle), 8 to 12 minutes.  
1. Remove pot from heat
1. Season with vinegar
